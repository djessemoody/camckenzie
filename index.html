Manuscript Evaluation

I will mark up your manuscript, but I won't change anything. This is less editing than direction for revisions and rewrites before a final line edit or copyedit. This option includes an hour discussing these suggestions with me via skype or in person if you're in the area.

Pace: 5-7 pgs./hr.

About

Words, when written down, have a certain level of permanence. They continue long after we do. They hold potential of explanation, learning, and insight into just exactly how deeply certain events can affect another human being. The written word offers an entrance into a microcosm of existence where we may be physically safe but are emotionally and intellectually vulnerable. Written words can teach us about others, but they can also teach us about ourselves--often more than we think is possible. But this is all simply potential until it can be realized. That movement from potentiality to reality is what drives me. In short, this is why I do what I do. As T.S. Eliot penned, "Between the idea / And the reality / Between the motion / And the act / Falls the Shadow."

Language, to me, is fascinating in all of its facets. Linguistics, rhetoric, the philosophy that influences, the theories of structure, the various means of interpretation, and the humor of a well-chosen pun all seem to demand that I continue asking not how it works, but why. I have studied language and literature, written stories and dissertations, taught rhetoric and persuasion, and in all of it, how we communicate to one another as humans has been the ultimate allure.

Throughout this pursuit, I have earned a B.A. in English with an emphasis in creative writing and an M.A.T. in Secondary English Education.â€‹ I have played guitar with a small jazz combo and used the concepts of "teaching" improvisation to examine how to teach creativity to my high school students, for which I received the John H. Lounsbury College of Education Achievements as Professional Educator Award (which is, indeed, a mouthful). I have published short stories and am writing my first novel. I have learned to utilize parallelism and anaphora in my writing for effect (and utilize meta-humor when I decide to be self-deprecating).

While reading and writing are two of my great loves, I also enjoy playing music, backpacking, making puns, and drinking all coffee in sight. It is also my opinion that nothing can beat a sunny day with Hemingway and a hammock, except for perhaps making silly faces at my newborn daughter and watching her smile.
